# Daily Workflow

This is the beginner-friendly GTM loop.

If you are an engineer and GTM feels fuzzy, use this document like a checklist.
You do not need to be a salesperson. The job right now is to learn from real
people with a repeatable process.

## The Point

Your goal is not to "do marketing."

Your goal is to:

- find founders who likely feel the pain
- send a short message
- get a few honest conversations
- learn whether the problem is real

Think of this like debugging demand.

## Default Daily Cadence

### Monday To Friday

1. Find `5-8` new companies
2. Qualify `3-5` founders
3. Score them with `lead-scoring-rubric.md`
4. Send `3-5` messages to the best leads
5. Update the sheet immediately
6. Write down anything surprising in `learnings.md`

That is enough. Consistency matters more than intensity.

## Step-By-Step

### Step 1: Find Companies

Use YC Companies first.

Look for:

- B2B SaaS
- workflow products
- devtools
- ops software
- integration-heavy products

Skip anything that feels clearly off-wedge.

### Step 2: Find The Founder

Use LinkedIn.

Find:

- Founder
- Co-Founder
- Founder & CEO
- CEO

You are not trying to find "important people."
You are trying to find the person who still likely feels the support pain.

### Step 3: Write The Reason

Before you add them to the outreach queue, write one sentence:

`Why do I believe this founder might feel the support-routing pain?`

If you cannot answer, the lead is not ready.

### Step 4: Score The Lead

Use `lead-scoring-rubric.md`.

Only message leads that score `8+` unless you are short on candidates.

### Step 5: Send A Short Message

Use `outreach-copy.md`.

Rules:

- short
- specific
- no pitch deck
- no demo ask
- lead with the pain, not the product

### Step 6: Track It

Update the tracker right away:

- DM sent
- reply
- call booked
- notes

Do not trust memory.

### Step 7: Learn

If someone replies with useful language, capture it in `learnings.md`.

This matters more than most people think. The exact words founders use are how
you improve both product and outreach.

## What A Good Week Looks Like

- `25-30` companies reviewed
- `15-20` qualified leads
- `10-15` messages sent
- `3-5` replies
- `2-3` calls
- at least one real pattern emerging

## What To Avoid

- messaging random founders with no thesis
- sending long paragraphs
- pitching too early
- changing the ICP every day
- assuming a polite response means real demand

## Mental Model For Engineers

This is not "sales" in the annoying sense.

It is closer to:

- creating a testable hypothesis
- gathering better input data
- finding repeated failure modes
- tightening the spec from reality

You are running a demand investigation loop.
