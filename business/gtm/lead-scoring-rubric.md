# Lead Scoring Rubric

This rubric exists to stop random outreach.

If you are unsure whether someone is a good lead, score them. The score forces
the reasoning into the open.

## How To Use It

Each lead gets a score from `0-10`.

- `8-10` = strong lead, message now
- `5-7` = maybe, keep if list is small
- `0-4` = skip

## The Five Checks

### 1. Company Type (`0-2`)

- `2` = clearly B2B SaaS or software product
- `1` = adjacent, but still plausibly fits
- `0` = consumer, agency, marketplace, or unclear

### 2. Team Size (`0-2`)

- `2` = likely `5-15` people
- `1` = probably `2-20`, but fuzzy
- `0` = clearly too big or too small for the current wedge

### 3. Founder Proximity To Customers (`0-2`)

- `2` = founder likely still close to support and user feedback
- `1` = possible, but not obvious
- `0` = likely too far from the day-to-day

### 4. Support Complexity (`0-2`)

- `2` = product likely creates questions, bugs, requests, or workflow confusion
- `1` = some support complexity, but not much
- `0` = unlikely to generate the kind of support pain we care about

### 5. Specific Pain Hypothesis (`0-2`)

- `2` = you can write one crisp sentence for why this founder probably feels the pain
- `1` = vague but plausible guess
- `0` = no concrete reason beyond "they are a founder"

## Example Of A Good Reason

`Founder at a 9-person workflow SaaS company with an integration-heavy product, so they likely still get pulled into customer questions and bug routing.`

## Example Of A Bad Reason

`Cool startup, founder seems smart.`

## Rule

If you cannot write the specific pain hypothesis, do not DM yet.

That usually means one of two things:

- the lead is weak
- you have not done enough homework
