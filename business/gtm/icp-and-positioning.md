# ICP And Positioning

## Core ICP

Founder-CEO at a `5-15` person B2B SaaS company who still handles customer
support in Slack and gets burned when they become the manual support router.

## Inclusion Criteria

- B2B SaaS company
- Small team, ideally `5-15` people
- Founder still close to customers
- Product likely generates support questions, bugs, and requests
- Team likely uses Slack internally

## Exclusion Criteria

- Consumer-first startups
- Agencies and services businesses
- Large startups where the founder is clearly far from support
- Companies with a mature support org already in place

## Pain Hypothesis

The founder is not looking for a full support platform yet. They are feeling
the cost of being the human routing layer:

- support requests interrupt focused work
- important customer context lives in Slack threads
- follow-up gets dropped or delayed
- the founder becomes a bottleneck for answers and next steps

## Positioning Hypothesis

TrustLoop is not a generic support platform for scaling teams.

TrustLoop helps a small B2B SaaS founder survive founder-led support in Slack
before they are ready for a full support stack.

## What We Need To Learn

- How painful is this right now?
- What do founders already do to cope?
- Which failure mode is sharpest: delay, dropped context, routing, or bug handoff?
- What language makes founders say "yes, that's exactly me"?
