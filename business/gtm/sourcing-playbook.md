# Sourcing Playbook

## Goal

Build a high-conviction list of founder leads who plausibly feel the current
 TrustLoop pain.

## Cheapest Good Stack

- YC Companies
- LinkedIn
- Google Sheet tracker

## Where To Look

### YC Companies

Search for companies that look like:

- B2B SaaS
- workflow-heavy
- integration-heavy
- developer tools
- ops software
- enterprise AI tools

Avoid:

- consumer apps
- agencies
- marketplaces unless clearly B2B workflow-heavy

### LinkedIn

For each company, find:

- `Founder`
- `Co-Founder`
- `Founder & CEO`
- `CEO`

Keep only founders where you can plausibly imagine they still get pulled into
customer support.

## Qualification Questions

Before adding a lead, answer:

1. Is this clearly a software company?
2. Is the team still small enough that the founder may touch support?
3. Does the product likely create support questions, requests, or bug reports?
4. Can I write one sentence for why this founder probably feels the pain?
5. Is there any public sign they may be trying to solve this now?

If the answer to `4` is no, skip the lead.

If the answer to `5` is yes, capture the evidence in the tracker. If the answer
is no, the lead can still stay, but it should not outrank a lead with real
intent evidence.

## Fit Score

Score from `0-10`:

- `+2` company looks like B2B SaaS
- `+2` team seems `5-15`
- `+2` founder likely still close to customers
- `+2` product likely creates support / request volume
- `+2` we can write a specific pain hypothesis

Only DM leads scoring `8+`.

## What Counts As "Already Looking"

You cannot read minds. Instead, look for observable behavior that suggests the
problem is active now.

Useful evidence:

- founder posts about support load, customer questions, onboarding friction, or bugs
- hiring for support, onboarding, implementation, or customer success
- new product pages or launches about inboxes, workflows, automations, or integrations
- recent funding or launch that likely increases support volume
- founder comments about spending too much time on customers or Slack

Bad evidence:

- they are in YC
- they raised money at some point
- they sell AI
- they liked a generic startup post

Output both:

- `fit`: why this company belongs in the wedge
- `intent`: why now might be a good time to message

## Weekly Target

- find `30` candidate companies
- qualify `30` founders
- DM top `10`
- book `3-5` calls
